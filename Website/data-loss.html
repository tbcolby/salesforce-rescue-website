Data Loss, Corruption, and Integrity Failures

Few Salesforce events create panic faster than data problems. One moment, everything seems fine. The next, critical records are gone, duplicates are multiplying, or reports show impossible numbers.

The stakes are immediate:

  • Sales can't close deals without accurate opportunity data
  • Service can't respond without customer history
  • Finance can't report without reliable numbers
  • Compliance obligations may be at risk

Common Data Crisis Triggers

Human Error

  • Accidental mass deletes – Wrong filter criteria, bulk action gone wrong
  • Faulty Data Loader imports – CSV errors, encoding issues, wrong object selected
  • Incorrect field mapping – Production data overwritten with sandbox data

System Issues

  • Duplicate record explosions – Matching rules disabled, batch jobs gone wild
  • Sandbox refresh issues – Production assumptions overwritten
  • Partial restore failures – Unclear gaps, relationship mismatches
  • Orphaned records – Relationships broken, reporting fails

Critical Reality:

Data issues rarely exist in isolation. They often coexist with automation and integration failures, which compounds the damage and makes diagnosis exponentially harder.

Our Data Recovery Approach

1. Containment First

Before recovery, we stop the bleeding. Identify what's still corrupting data and shut it down immediately — even if it means disabling automation or pausing integrations.

2. Forensic Analysis

Determine the scope: What was affected? When did it start? What's the blast radius? We use Setup Audit Trail, Field History, API logs, and bulk query analysis to piece together what happened.

3. Surgical Recovery

Not all data needs to be restored. We identify critical vs. recoverable-later records, prioritize business-critical objects, and execute targeted recovery with validation at every step.

4. Prevention Architecture

Once stabilized, we implement guardrails: validation rules, process automation, backup strategies, and documentation so this specific failure mode can't happen again.

Why Speed Matters

Every minute of delay:

  • Allows bad data to propagate to downstream systems
  • Makes forensic analysis harder (logs age out)
  • Increases user confusion and workarounds
  • Erodes trust in the system
